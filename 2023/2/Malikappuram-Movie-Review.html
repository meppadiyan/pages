Malikappuram Movie Review

A simple story that turned out to be a Giant Slayer

Posted by self on Thu, 9 Feb 2023
I want to watch this again with my family. I want to show this to my daughter.
These were the exact words my North Indian friend said as we got out of the theatre.

As Unni Mukundan in one of his interviews rightly said, watching movies from home is a personal affair, but, theatre experience is a social affair. And you cannot deny that on yourself for too long, coz, man IS a social animal. You strive to know how others feel. No wonder reaction channels are so popular. So when I witnessed the standing ovation at the end of the movie, which is very rare in this part of the world, I said to myself - “You came for this”. Shared joy is double joy. Shared sorrow is half sorrow.

The Plot

To me, the plot is nothing more than how a little girl makes her way into the world to achieve what she wants. More than how she achieves it, it’s more about what. There is a terrific combination of innocence and maturity that not many movies have explored. I’m probably at a risk of being called a moron if I tell you that Malikappuram gave me Leon type vibes.

Malikappuram, like Leon tells a story of a young girl’s determination and how far they are willing to go to make things right.

Both movies traverse through a tender line.
