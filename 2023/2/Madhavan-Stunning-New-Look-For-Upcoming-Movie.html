Who is C Shankaran Nair ?

Chettur Sankaran Nair, also known as C. Sankaran Nair, was an Indian lawyer, politician, and freedom fighter who played a significant role in India's struggle for independence from British rule. He was born on October 12, 1857, in Ottapalam, a town in present-day Kerala, India.

C. Sankaran Nair was educated in India and in the United Kingdom, where he studied law at the Inner Temple in London. He became a successful lawyer and was appointed as a judge in the Madras High Court in 1905. He was later appointed as a member of the Viceroy's Executive Council, which was the highest governing body in British India.

However, he resigned from his post in protest against the British government's handling of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919, which was a turning point in India's struggle for independence. He became actively involved in the Indian National Congress and worked closely with Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters.

After the incident, C Shankaran Nair went to England and took Jallianwalabagh massacre to courtHe was also a critic of Gandhi and in his book “Gandhi and Anarchy” he writes ‘Non-cooperation as advocated by Mr Gandhi may be a weapon to be used when constitutional methods have failed to achieve our purpose. Non-violence and passive suffering will lead to bloodshed or be unfruitful of any satisfactory results.’
